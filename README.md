# Energy & Finance Cross‑Analysis: Germany–France Benchmark

A benchmark of two of Europe’s largest energy systems. The project integrates financial and energy‑sector data, analysing company betas, CAPEX, electricity prices, bond spreads, and production structure to assess the resilience and efficiency of both models.

# project-description

The aim of this project is to highlight the key differences between two energy production models: the French and the German. The French model is built primarily on nuclear power, while the German model is transitional in nature — following the phase‑out of nuclear energy, Germany has shifted toward a mix dominated by renewable sources and natural gas.

The analysis covers three major energy companies and their market and financial data: France’s EDF and Germany’s RWE and E.ON. The project also examines electricity market prices in both countries, as well as the evolution of their respective energy production mixes.

From the financial perspective, the study focuses on:

- beta, as a measure of market risk,

- cost of debt, expressed through bond spreads,

- capital investments (CAPEX), reflecting changes in infrastructure investment levels.
